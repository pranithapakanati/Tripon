Frequently asked questions

01

What is the best time of year to visit Bali?

The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season from April to October, when weather is sunny and rainfall is minimal. The peak season is July to August, while April, May, and June offer fewer crowds with pleasant weather. December to March is the rainy season but offers lower prices and lush green landscapes.

02

What are the must-see attractions in Bali?

Must-see attractions in Bali include Tanah Lot Temple (iconic clifftop temple), Ubud (cultural heart with arts and crafts), Mount Batur (sunrise trekking), Tegallalang Rice Terraces, Tirta Empul Temple (sacred water temple), Seminyak Beach, Nusa Penida Islands, and the Bali Swing. Each offers unique experiences of Bali's natural beauty and rich heritage.

03

Do I need a visa to visit Bali?

Most nationalities can obtain a Visa on Arrival (VOA) at Bali's Ngurah Rai International Airport for USD $35, valid for 30 days. Alternatively, many countries qualify for Indonesia's free 30-day exemption. Some nationalities need an e-Visa obtained online before arrival. Check with the Indonesian embassy for your specific country requirements.

01

What types of accommodations are available in Bali?

Bali offers diverse accommodation options including luxury resorts in Seminyak and Nusa Dua, beachfront villas in Canggu, traditional guesthouses in Ubud, eco-lodges in mountain areas, and budget-friendly hostels throughout the island. Each area has its own character—beach areas are perfect for relaxation, while Ubud suits cultural immersion and mountain regions offer adventure.

02

Which areas of Bali are best for families?

Seminyak, Kuta, and Nusa Dua are excellent for families with safe beaches, family-friendly resorts, and plenty of activities. Ubud offers cultural experiences and nature adventures. Water sports, animal sanctuaries (like the Bali Bird Park), and theme parks like Bali Safari & Marine Park provide entertainment for children. Beach clubs often have kid-friendly facilities.

03

What is the average cost of accommodation in Bali?

Budget hostels range from $10-25 per night, mid-range hotels from $40-100, and luxury resorts from $150-500+. Villas offer great value and privacy. Prices vary by location and season—popular areas like Seminyak are pricier than Ubud or Sanur. Booking in advance or visiting during low season can save 20-30% on accommodation costs.

01

How do I get around Bali?

Getting around Bali is easy with options including car rentals with drivers (recommended for safety), motorbike rentals, ride-sharing apps (Gojek, Grab), taxis, and shuttle buses. Many tourists hire a driver for the day for flexibility and reliability. Renting a motorbike is budget-friendly but requires a valid international license and comfort with local traffic.

02

What is the best way to travel between Bali's regions?

The island is compact, making travel convenient. Seminyak to Ubud takes 90 minutes by car, Ubud to Mount Batur 1.5 hours, and Seminyak to Nusa Penida involves a scenic ferry ride. Hiring a private driver for day trips is comfortable and affordable (around $40-60). Alternatively, join organized tours that include transportation and guided experiences at major attractions.

03

How long should I spend in Bali?

A minimum of 3-4 days allows you to experience beaches, cultural sites, and nature. A week-long trip is ideal to explore multiple regions like Seminyak, Ubud, and mountain areas without rushing. Two weeks lets you deeply immerse in Bali's culture, do adventure activities, and relax on beaches. Even 5-6 days gives a solid overview of the island's highlights.

01

What activities and experiences are available in Bali?

Bali offers a wide range of experiences including water sports (surfing, diving, snorkeling), temple visits and spiritual ceremonies, rice terrace trekking, Mount Batur sunrise hikes, cooking classes, traditional Balinese dance performances, yoga retreats, spa treatments, and wildlife encounters. Adventure seekers can try paragliding, white-water rafting, and jungle canopy walks.

02

Are there any cultural festivals or events in Bali?

Bali celebrates several important festivals including Nyepi (Balinese New Year, March-April), Odalan (temple anniversary celebrations held throughout the year), and Bali Arts Festival (June-July). During these events, witness traditional ceremonies, vibrant processions, and cultural performances. Visiting during festivals provides authentic cultural immersion and colorful experiences unique to Balinese traditions.

03

What local cuisines should I try in Bali?

Must-try Balinese dishes include Nasi Campur (mixed rice with sides), Satay (grilled meat skewers with peanut sauce), Gado-Gado (vegetable salad with peanut dressing), and fresh seafood. Visit local warungs (small restaurants) for authentic flavors. Take a cooking class in Ubud to learn traditional recipes. Don't miss Bali coffee, fresh tropical fruits, and Bintang beer.
